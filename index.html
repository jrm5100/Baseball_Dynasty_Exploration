Dynasties in MLB

What is a Dynasty?

A sports dynasty is a team that dominates their competitors for an extended length of time. One possible definition for a dynasty in the MLB (major league baseball) is a team that wins 3 or more championships in 5 years. By this definition there have been 9 dynasties in the 130+ year history of the league. This visualization allows a more in depth look at the statistics behind the success of these teams.

The data comes from Lahman's Baseball Database. The first graph displays one statistic for active franchises with the selected dynasty highlighted. The second graph displays two statistics in a scatterplot for the selected year of the dynasty. The statistics displayed in each graph can be customized. Offensive and defensive statistics (runs, strikeouts, errors, etc) have been normalized where appropriate so that they match the modern 162 game season.

It's also possible to define a custom dynasty by selecting a team and a range of seasons. For example, the Atlanta Braves made 14 consecutive postseasons starting in 1991, but only won 1 World Series in that time.
